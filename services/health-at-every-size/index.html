Body Liberation & Food Freedom

Health at Every Size & Intuitive Eating

Traditional wellness culture has spent decades telling people in larger bodies that weight loss is the path to health, happiness, and worth. The research doesn't back that up. And the psychological cost of that message is enormous. We offer a different approach — one that starts with the radical premise that you deserve care exactly as you are.

HAES-aligned, Intuitive Eating-informed therapy — in Denver & online across Colorado

A Different Way of Thinking About Health

Health at Every Size® (HAES) is a framework that shifts the focus from weight management to holistic wellbeing. It recognizes that bodies naturally come in a wide range of shapes and sizes, that weight is not a reliable indicator of health, and that shame and restriction tend to make things worse — not better.

At Full Bloom, we work from a HAES framework because we believe people deserve care that doesn't center their body size. That's not a political stance. It's what the evidence supports — and it's what our clients actually need.

Intuitive Eating: Coming Home to Your Body

Intuitive Eating is a framework developed by registered dietitians Evelyn Tribole and Elyse Resch. It has nothing to do with eating whatever you want, all the time — and everything to do with dismantling the chronic cycle of restriction, guilt, and compensating that keeps so many people stuck.

Our certified Intuitive Eating counselors work with clients to reconnect with hunger and fullness cues, challenge the internalized voice of diet culture, and find genuine pleasure and satisfaction in eating — without the noise.

For more on the HAES® principles, visit HAES Community.

What Weight-Inclusive Therapy Looks Like

Our therapists are trained in HAES and Intuitive Eating and hold these principles throughout our work — not just when discussing food explicitly. What this means in practice:

◯

No Weight Goals

We never set weight loss as a treatment goal or measure progress by body size. Progress looks like reduced anxiety around food, improved relationship with your body, greater freedom.

◈

Trauma-Informed

Food and body issues rarely exist in a vacuum. We explore the experiences — family messages, cultural pressure, past trauma — that shaped your relationship with your body.

⟳

Collaborative Care

When appropriate, we coordinate with registered dietitians and other providers who share our HAES-aligned values for a full-team approach.

Who This Work Is For

You don't need a diagnosis to work with us. We see clients navigating chronic dieting, body dissatisfaction, binge-restrict cycles, emotional eating, and the particular exhaustion that comes from living in a body you've been at war with for years. We also work with clients in recovery from clinical eating disorders.

If you're looking for a Denver therapist who won't put you on a scale — metaphorically or literally — reach out for a free consultation.

Common Questions

HAES challenges the idea that intentional weight loss is necessary for health or worth constant pursuit — that's different from telling anyone what to do with their body. What we explore in therapy is your relationship with your body and food, and whether your current approach is genuinely caring or quietly punishing.

Intuitive Eating has 10 principles focused on reconnecting with internal cues rather than external food rules. It's been associated with improved psychological wellbeing and reduced disordered eating behaviors in research. It's not permission to eat whatever you want — it's rebuilding trust with yourself around food.

No referral is required, and you don't need a clinical diagnosis. If your relationship with food is causing distress or taking up significant mental space, our therapists can help — regardless of where you fall on the clinical spectrum.
