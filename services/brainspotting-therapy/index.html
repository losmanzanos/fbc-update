Anxiety Therapy

Brainspotting reaches what talking can't. Trauma, anxiety, and stuck emotional states live in the subcortical brain — below language, below conscious thought. Brainspotting works there directly, using your eye position to locate and process material that talk therapy alone can't always touch.

How Brainspotting Works

Your brain stores unprocessed trauma in the subcortical regions — the parts that operate below language and conscious reasoning. These are the same regions responsible for survival responses: freeze, fight, flight. Traditional talk therapy works primarily at the cortical level — insight, language, meaning-making. Brainspotting works deeper.

When you hold your gaze at a specific eye position — a "brainspot" — while staying attuned to what's happening in your body, the brain processes the stored material on its own. Your therapist doesn't direct what comes up. They hold a steady, attuned presence while your brain does what it was built to do: heal.

Brainspotting vs. EMDR

Both are brain-based trauma therapies. The key difference is structure.

✦

EMDR uses a structured protocol with deliberate bilateral stimulation — moving back and forth across the brain in measured sets. Highly effective for trauma with clear targets.

✦

Brainspotting is more organic. Once a spot is located, the client holds their gaze while the brain processes freely. Often reaches deeper or more diffuse material.

Many clients do both — SSP to build nervous system capacity, EMDR for specific trauma targets, Brainspotting for what remains. Your therapist will help you figure out what fits where.

What Brainspotting Can Help With

✦

Trauma & PTSD — Especially material that feels pre-verbal, body-based, or resistant to EMDR

✦

Anxiety — Including anxiety without a clear cognitive cause, driven by subcortical fear structures

✦

Addiction & compulsions — Processing the emotional material underneath compulsive behavior

✦

Somatic complaints — Chronic pain, tension, or fatigue that tracks alongside emotional states

✦

Performance anxiety — Athletes, performers, and professionals whose nervous systems interfere with their abilities

✦

Grief & loss — Particularly grief that feels stuck or has been held for a long time without moving
