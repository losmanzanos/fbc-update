EMDR Therapy in Denver

EMDR helps the brain process painful memories that got stuck. Not by talking them to death — by working directly with how they're stored. If something from your past is still shaping how you feel, think, or move through the world today, EMDR may be exactly what's been missing.

How EMDR Works

Developed by Francine Shapiro, EMDR uses bilateral stimulation — guided eye movements, taps, or tones — to help the brain reprocess distressing memories. The goal isn't to erase what happened. It's to help it feel like the past, rather than something you're still living through.

EMDR is recognized by the World Health Organization, APA, and VA as a frontline treatment for PTSD. Over decades of research, it's proven effective for anxiety, depression, phobias, grief, and negative self-beliefs.

What to Expect in a Session

EMDR feels different from talk therapy. You don't have to describe everything in detail. Your therapist will help you identify a target memory, establish safety resources, then guide you through bilateral stimulation while you notice what arises — images, sensations, thoughts, emotions.

Many clients describe it as surprisingly calm. Most say it's nothing like what they expected — and that they notice shifts faster than they anticipated. Your therapist moves at a pace your nervous system can tolerate the whole way through.

What EMDR Can Help With

Not just single-incident trauma — EMDR reaches a wide range of concerns:

✦

Trauma & PTSD — Single-incident or complex, recent or long-ago

✦

Anxiety & Panic — When triggers feel disproportionate to what's actually happening

✦

Depression — Particularly when rooted in past experiences or chronic shame

✦

Grief & Loss — When grief won't move through on its own

✦

Phobias — Reducing the charge of specific triggers

✦

Negative self-beliefs — "I'm not safe," "I'm worthless," "It was my fault"
