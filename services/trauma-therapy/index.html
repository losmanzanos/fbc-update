Heal What Holds You Back

Trauma Therapy

Trauma doesn't always look like crisis. It can look like a body that can't relax, a mind that won't stop scanning for danger, relationships that keep going sideways in familiar ways. You know something is off. You've known for a long time. Healing is possible — and it doesn't require reliving everything.

70% of adults experience at least one traumatic event in their lifetime
3–4 evidence-based modalities offered at Full Bloom for trauma
Free 15-minute consultation — no commitment required

Who We Work With

Our therapists support adults navigating many different trauma experiences. You don't have to have a formal PTSD diagnosis to work with us — if something from your past is still affecting your present, that's enough.

  • ✦Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) & Complex PTSD
  • ✦Childhood & developmental trauma
  • ✦Relational trauma & attachment wounds
  • ✦Grief & loss after traumatic events
  • ✦Flashbacks, hypervigilance, emotional numbness

Our Trauma Therapy Approaches

We use evidence-based modalities that work at the level of the body and nervous system — not just insight and conversation.

◉

EMDR Therapy

Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing. Uses bilateral stimulation to help the brain reprocess stuck traumatic memories. Recognized by the WHO and APA as a frontline treatment for PTSD.

♬

Safe & Sound Protocol

SSP uses filtered music to help regulate the autonomic nervous system. It's often used as foundational work before trauma processing begins — building the felt sense of safety needed for deeper healing.

◯

Somatic Therapy

Trauma lives in the body. Somatic awareness, breathwork, and nervous system regulation techniques help you notice — and gradually shift — the physical residue of what you've been carrying.

Trauma-informed care in Denver & online across Colorado

Your Healing, Your Pace

We never push. We never rush. Good trauma therapy begins with building safety — the physiological and relational sense that this is a space where your nervous system can eventually settle. That sometimes takes weeks. It's not a sign that things aren't working.

When you're ready, we move into processing. When you're not, we stay in stabilization. Your window of tolerance guides everything. Most clients find that progress comes faster than they expected — and that it feels different from anything they'd tried before.

"I'd been to three other therapists before Full Bloom. This is the first time I've actually felt a shift — not just insight, but something changing in my body."
